General Information about Forest

Forests are vital ecosystems that play a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance, supporting biodiversity, and providing numerous benefits to humanity. They cover a significant portion of the Earth's land surface and are characterized by a high density of trees and other vegetation.

Key functions of forests include:

  • Carbon Sequestration: Forests absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, helping to mitigate climate change.
  • Biodiversity Conservation: They provide habitat for a vast array of plant and animal species, contributing to the preservation of biodiversity.
  • Water Cycle Regulation: Forests play a key role in the water cycle, influencing rainfall patterns, preventing soil erosion, and maintaining water quality.
  • Resource Provision: They supply timber, fuelwood, and non-timber forest products such as medicinal plants, fruits, and resins, supporting livelihoods and economies.
  • Recreation and Tourism: Forests offer opportunities for recreation, tourism, and cultural activities, contributing to human well-being.

About Thammampatty Forest Range

The Thammampatty Forest Range was established on 01.09.1990, carved out from the Rasipuram Range. Prior to its formation as a separate range, it functioned as a section under the Rasipuram Range. This restructuring aimed to improve the management and conservation efforts in the region.

Pachamalai Hills

The Pachamalai Hills fall under the jurisdiction of the Thammampatty Forest Range. These hills are an important geographical feature, known for their biodiversity, unique ecosystem, and scenic beauty. The hills support a variety of flora and fauna and play a crucial role in the local climate and water resources.

Attur Division

The Thammampatty Forest Range is part of the Attur Forest Division. The division is responsible for the administration and management of the forests within its jurisdiction, including the Thammampatty Range.

Forest Range Officer

The present Forest Range Officer of Thammampatty Range is Mr. D. Murugesan. He is responsible for overseeing the management and conservation activities within the range.
